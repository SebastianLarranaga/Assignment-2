- INTRODUCTION -

Following the COVID-19 pandemic, the WHO (World Health Organization) declared that the next global threat as being superbugs, which are a form of bacteria that become resistant to antibiotics often produced by living organisms.

The Global Research on Antimicrobial Resistance, otherwise known as GRAM, is a leading authority in the world of antibiotic resistance research. They carried out a study in over 150 countries that predicts that by 2050, over 39 million globally will have died as a result of the excessive use of antibiotics, including last resort treatments for bacteria that will have adapted to them by then.

This threat looms over the head of the scientific health community, and despite the warnings, people don't seem to be worried.
